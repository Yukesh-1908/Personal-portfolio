About

Where it started, and where it's going.

I've been drawn to computers for as long as I can remember. As a kid, it wasn't the games that pulled me in — it was the need to know what was happening underneath them. That curiosity never really went away; it just found better outlets.

Somewhere along the way I discovered Linux, and it changed how I look at systems entirely. Distros like Linux Mint and Kali Linux gave me a way to actually understand what an operating system is doing instead of just using it. I'm currently working my way toward Arch Linux, partly because I want to configure a system from the ground up, and partly because I like doing things the hard way once, so I understand them properly.

Right now, I'm a second-year Computer Science Engineering student specializing in AI & Machine Learning at Jeppiaar Engineering College, Anna University. I'm early in my machine learning journey — learning the fundamentals, working through NumPy and Pandas, and building small projects to turn theory into something real.

My goal is straightforward: become a Machine Learning Engineer who understands systems deeply enough to build things that actually work, not just things that demo well.
